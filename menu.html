Traditional Māori Food:

One of the staple foods in Māori cuisine is the kai moana, or seafood, sourced from the abundant coastal waters surrounding the islands. Fish, such as snapper and blue cod, along with shellfish like green-lipped mussels and tuatua (a type of clam), play a significant role in Māori meals. The preparation of these ingredients often involves traditional cooking methods like hangi, where food is buried in an earth oven with hot stones, creating a distinct smoky flavor. Additionally, the gathering of kai moana holds cultural importance, reinforcing the Māori connection to the land and sea.

Another essential component of Māori cuisine is the use of indigenous flora and fauna. The kumara, a sweet potato variety, holds a special place in Māori culture and diet. Its cultivation dates back centuries, and it is prepared in various ways, such as boiling, roasting, or in soups. The hāngī-cooked meats, including lamb, pork, and chicken, are often accompanied by a variety of root vegetables like potatoes and taro, creating a hearty and flavorful meal. The integration of traditional Māori ingredients not only sustains the body but also serves as a means of preserving cultural practices and passing them down through generations.

Rewena Bread Recipe

Rewena Bread Rewena (Maori bread) is a favourite and goes well with many meals or on its own as a snack. The recipe is simple however quantities and timing can take time to master. The first part of the recipe is the making of a ‘bug’ (starter) that helps the bread rise without yeast.

Bug (Starter):

  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  1. Boil the potato in water (without salt) until soft and leave to cool.
  2. When lukewarm, mash the potato and water and mix in the flour and sugar.
  3. Add more warm water if needed to make a batter.
  4. Cover and leave in a warm place to rise for a day – the batter will smell yeasty and have small bubbles on the surface.

Bread:

  • 5 cups flour
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon baking soda
  • 1 to 2 teaspoons of sugar
  • 1 cup of your rewena bug
  1. Mix flour and salt into a large bowl.
  2. Make a well in the center.
  3. Place the rewena bug in the middle of the hole and sprinkle baking soda over all.
  4. Knead lightly for 10 to 15 minutes, folding the dough over and over.
  5. Add more water if the mixture feels too firm.
  6. Add sugar to sweeten according to your taste.
  7. Roll into a ball, cover, and leave to rise for a few hours or overnight.
  8. To make the dough rise faster, leave it in the warming drawer of the oven or on a shelf in the sun.
  9. Sprinkle flour over a baking tray to prevent sticking and lightly spray the bread with liquid oil.
  10. Bake at 200 degrees Celsius for approximately 40 minutes.
